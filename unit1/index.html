Greetings

Hello (the most common greeting expression)

Hi (informal greeting)

Good morning (said from sunrise to noon)

Good afternoon (said from noon to 5/6 pm)

Good evening (said from 5/6 pm to midnight)

(It’s) nice to meet you (said when you meet someone for the first time)

How are you? (neutral expression)

How is it going? (informal expression)

What’s up? (casual expression)

Practice the following dialogue with your partners:

Tour Guide: Hello everyone, I am your tour guide for today. Good morning!

Jack: Hi there! How are you?

Tour Guide: I'm great, thanks for asking.

Marie: And what's your name?

Tour Guide: My name is Sarah. What about you?

Marie: I'm Marie and this is my husband Jack.

Sarah: Nice to meet you both. Where are you from?

Jack: We're from Canada.

Sarah: Oh, I love Canada. It's a beautiful country.

Marie: Thank you. So, what's the plan for the tour today?

Sarah: First, we'll visit the local market and then head to the historical sites in the city center. After that, we'll take a boat ride along the river.

Jack: Sounds great! We've been looking forward to this tour.

Sarah: That's wonderful to hear. Let's get started!

Filling out a form

Watch the video with a dialogue between Sarah and the receptionist at San Diego International School.

Receptionist: Good afternoon! Welcome to San Diego International School. How may I help you?

Sarah: Oh, good afternoon. My name is Sarah and I want to enroll in the intermediate English course. Sorry, what’s your name?

Receptionist: Excellent! My name is Andrea and I will help you with that. It’s very nice to meet you, Sarah.

Sarah: Nice to meet you, too.

Receptionist: Now, I will need some information to complete your registration. So, your first name is Sarah. How do you spell it?

Sarah: It’s S-A-R-A-H.

Receptionist: Okay. And what’s your last name?

Sarah: My last name is Rodrigues.

Receptionist: Can you spell that, please?

Sarah: Oh, yes. It’s R-O-D-R-I-G-U-E-S.

Receptionist: How old are you, Sarah?

Sarah: I’m thirty-seven years old.

Receptionist: Okay, thirty-seven. And what’s your nationality?

Sarah: I’m Brazilian. I’m from Porto Alegre.

Receptionist: That’s great. I have been to Brazil once and I love it! What’s your address in Brazil?

Sarah: It’s four five zero, Ipiranga Avenue. Apartment six zero three.

Receptionist: And what’s your zip code?

Sarah: It’s 90610 100.

Receptionist: Alright. Please give me your telephone number.

Sarah: Sure. It’s 55 (that’s the code for Brazil) 51-99896-8829.

Receptionist: Okay. 55-51-99896-8829. And what’s your passport number?

Sarah: It’s XYZ9791.

Receptionist: Sorry, could you repeat that, please?

Sarah: Yes. It’s XYZ9791.

Receptionist: Thank you. What’s your occupation, Sarah?

Sarah: I’m a lawyer.

Receptionist: Are you married?

Sarah: No, I’m single.

Receptionist: Finally, can you tell me your email address?

Sarah: Yes. My email address is sarah_rodrigues@mail.com

Receptionist: Okay, that’s all. You are now enrolled in the intermediate English course. You will receive all the information you need in your email.

Sarah: Thank you, Andrea. Goodbye.

Receptionist: Bye Sarah. Have a good day!
