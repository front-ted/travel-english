Dialogue 1

Lucy (at the mall): Excuse me. Can you help me? I`m lost!

Jack: Sure! Where do you want to go?

Lucy: I’m looking for the Museum of Modern Art.

Jack: Oh, that`s easy! Just go straight, turn left on St. Patrick’s Street, then turn right on Pearl Avenue, walk about one hundred meters and turn right on Broadway Street. The Museum of Modern Art is on the left.

Lucy: Ok, thanks a lot!

Jack: No problem. Enjoy the museum!

Dialogue 2

John: Hi! Excuse me. Can you help me? Where is the nearest beach?

Bryan: Of course! I’d be happy to help. The nearest beach is on Main Street, opposite the mall.

John: Thank you very much. I appreciate your help.

Bryan: You’re welcome! Have a good day!

Transportation in Cape Town, South Africa

What do you know about South Africa?

Would you like to travel to South Africa? Why (not)?

Read the following article about transportation in Cape Town and then take the quiz to check your comprehension.

Cape Town, a beautiful city in South Africa, offers various ways for people to move around. Here are some common means of transportation:

Buses: In Cape Town, colorful buses travel around the city, making it easy for people to get to different places.

Mini-Taxis: Small taxis, often painted in bright colors, are a popular way to travel short distances, especially in busy areas.

Trains: Cape Town has a train system that connects different parts of the city, providing a reliable mode of transportation.

MyCiTi Bus: This is a special bus service in Cape Town that follows specific routes and stops at designated stations, making commuting convenient.

Walking: Many people enjoy walking in Cape Town, especially around the city center or along the picturesque waterfront.

Bicycles: Some areas in Cape Town are bicycle-friendly, allowing people to ride bikes for short journeys.

Cars: Like in other places, cars are widely used in Cape Town for personal transportation, offering flexibility to explore the city and its surroundings.

Cable Car: To enjoy breathtaking views of Cape Town, visitors can take a cable car up to Table Mountain, a famous landmark.

Uber/Taxis: Ride-sharing services like Uber and traditional taxis are available for those who prefer a more private mode of transportation.

Boats/Ferries: In the coastal areas of Cape Town, boats and ferries provide a scenic way to travel and explore the surrounding waters.
