Telling the time

Learn how to tell the time in English.

Examples:

2:00 – It's two o’clock.

2:05 – It's five past two.

2:10 – It's ten past two.

2:15 – It's (a) quarter past two.

2:25 – It's twenty-five past two.

2:30 – It's half past two.

2:35 – It's twenty-five to three.

2:40 – It's twenty to three.

2:45 – It's (a) quarter to three.

2:50 – It's ten to three.

2:55 – It's five to three.

Important note:

We use AT + TIME when giving the time of a specific event.

Examples:

Our class starts at six o’clock.

The train leaves the station at ten past eleven.

We use IT IS or IT’S to answer a question that asks for the time right now.

Examples:

What time is it? It’s five to nine.

What’s the time? It’s twelve o’clock.

  • What time do you have English class? I have English class from _____ to _____.

Ordering food at a restaurant

Listen to the dialogue between the waiter and two guests at Flavors.

Transcrição

Waiter: Good evening and welcome to Flavors! How are you doing?

Samantha: We are fine, thank you. And you? Can we see the menu, please?

Waiter: I’m fine, too, madam. Certainly, here is the menu.

Samantha: Thank you.

Waiter: Excuse me. Are you ready to order?

Joseph: Humm, I have some questions.

Waiter: Certainly, sir. How may I help you?

Joseph: Are there any lactose-free options?

Waiter: Of course. For appetizers, I recommend the fresh salad, and then the octopus with vegetables is an excellent lactose-free option.

Joseph: Excellent. I’ll have a fresh salad and the octopus with vegetables.

Waiter: Alright. And for you, madam?

Samantha: I’m a vegetarian, so I’ll have a fresh salad and the pasta with tomato and broccoli.

Waiter: Anything to drink?

Samantha: I’d like a glass of red wine, please.

Joseph: And I’ll have an orange juice.

Waiter: Great choices. I will serve your meal shortly.

Waiter: Thank you. Here you are. Enjoy your meal!

Joseph: Thank you.

Waiter: Can I get you anything else?

Samantha: Could I have a glass of water, please?

Waiter: Certainly, madam.

Waiter: Would you like to order some dessert?

Samantha: Hummm. What do you recommend?

Waiter: Our chocolate ice-cream is very popular. And the cupcake is vegan and lactose-free.

Samantha: Great, I’ll take the chocolate ice-cream.

Joseph: And I’ll have a chocolate and strawberry cupcake, please.

Waiter: Would you like some tea or coffee?

Samantha: No, thank you.

Joseph: I would like a cup of coffee, please.

(Pausa – ruidos de restaurante - garçom retorna à mesa)

Joseph: I’d like the check, please.

Waiter: Sure. That will be eighty-one dollars.

Samantha: Here you are. Keep the change.

Waiter: Thank you! Have a good day!

Joseph: Goodbye!
